QUESTION TYPE: HEMATOLOGY
CLINICAL CASE: 
In a 29-year-old woman the following parameters are found in the course of a routine blood test: Hb 11.5 g/dL, MCV 70 fl, MCH 28 pg, Ferritin 10 ng/mL, leukocytes 5,200/mm3, platelets 335,000/mm3. Physical examination is normal. The most indicated examination in this situation is:
1- Gynecological examination.
2- Fecal occult blood test.
3- Radiological study of the digestive tract.
4- Hemoglobin electrophoresis.
5- Cooms test.
CORRECT ANSWER: 1
The most frequent cause of microcytic iron deficiency anemia in young women is chronic gynecologic leaks.

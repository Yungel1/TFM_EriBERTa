QUESTION TYPE: CRITICAL, PALLIATIVE AND EMERGENCY CARE
CLINICAL CASE: 
A 35-year-old man suffers a high-voltage electrical burn (3000 volts) from direct contact with wire with his left hand. On admission he presents flexion contracture of the hand, pallor of the fingers and absence of radial and ulnar pulse on palpation What is the emergency invasive measure to be performed?
1- Axillary block with catheter.
2- Decompressive scarotomy.
3- Intracompartmental pressure monitoring.
4- Scarectomy.
5- nan
CORRECT ANSWER: 3
3. In the case of suspected compartment syndrome, the urgent invasive measure to perform would be to measure the intracompartmental pressure and, depending on the result, proceed with treatment.
